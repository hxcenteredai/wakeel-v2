Article (2)
Commercial activities include all acts performed by a trader for the purposes of
his business, and all acts of trade defined under this Law, regardless of the
status of the persons performing them. Trading in goods, services, and the
provision of professional commercial services for consideration fall within the
scope of this Law.

Article (70)
Parties to a commercial contract shall perform their obligations in good faith
and in a manner consistent with the customary practices of the trade. The
parties shall not exercise their rights in a manner that is intended to harm the
other party or that exceeds what is necessary to protect a legitimate
commercial interest.

Article (87)
A trader who, by reason of his profession, becomes aware of confidential
information concerning the business of another party shall keep that
information secret and shall not disclose it to any third party except with the
written consent of the owner of the information or pursuant to an order of a
competent court. Disclosure or use of such information in breach of this
obligation entitles the injured party to compensation.

Article (91)
A party who fails to perform a commercial obligation, or who delays performance,
shall be liable for the damages caused to the other party by reason of such
failure or delay, unless he proves that the non-performance was due to a cause
beyond his control. Damages shall include both the loss suffered and the loss
of profit, provided they are a direct result of the non-performance.

Article (379)
Trade secrets, including manufacturing methods, customer lists, pricing
formulae, and other commercial information of economic value that is not
generally known and that the holder has taken reasonable steps to keep
confidential, shall be protected against unlawful acquisition, use, or
disclosure by any party, including employees, contractors, and counterparties
to a commercial agreement.

Article (396)
Where parties to a commercial agreement have expressly agreed on duties of
confidentiality, such duties shall be construed in accordance with the express
terms of the agreement, supplemented by the general duties of good faith and
trade custom under this Law. In the absence of an express term, confidentiality
obligations relating to non-public commercial information shall survive
termination of the commercial relationship for a reasonable period.
