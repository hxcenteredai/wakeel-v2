Article (1)
Definitions. The following terms shall have the meanings assigned thereto:
Personal Data, Sensitive Personal Data, Processing, Controller, Processor, Data
Subject, Consent, Cross-Border Transfer, and the UAE Data Office, as defined for
the purposes of this Decree-Law.

Article (4)
Personal Data may not be processed without the consent of the Data Subject,
except in the cases stipulated in this Decree-Law, including where processing is
necessary for the performance of a contract, compliance with a legal obligation,
or the protection of the vital interests of the Data Subject.

Article (5)
Personal Data shall be processed in a fair, transparent and lawful manner; it
shall be collected for specified, clear and legitimate purposes; and it shall be
accurate, complete and kept up to date, and limited to what is necessary for the
purposes of processing.

Article (7)
Where processing is based on consent, the Controller must be able to demonstrate
that the Data Subject has given consent to the processing of Personal Data. The
Data Subject may withdraw consent at any time, and withdrawal shall be as easy as
giving consent. Consent must be given by a clear affirmative act, freely, in a
specific and unambiguous manner.

Article (9)
The Data Subject shall have the right to request information about the categories
of Personal Data processed, the purposes of processing, the recipients to whom
data is or will be disclosed, and the mechanisms for cross-border transfer.

Article (13)
The Data Subject shall have the right to request the erasure of their Personal
Data held by the Controller, subject to the exceptions set out in this
Decree-Law, including retention required for compliance with a legal obligation.

Article (22)
The Controller shall notify the UAE Data Office of any breach of Personal Data
that would prejudice the privacy, confidentiality or security of the data of the
Data Subject, upon becoming aware of such breach, together with the results of
any investigation.

Article (23)
Personal Data may be transferred outside the State to jurisdictions that ensure
an adequate level of protection. In the absence of an adequacy decision, transfer
is permitted subject to appropriate safeguards, the explicit consent of the Data
Subject, or the other conditions stipulated in this Decree-Law.
