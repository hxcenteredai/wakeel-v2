Article (1)
Definitions. In application of the provisions of this Decree-Law, the following
words and expressions shall have the meanings assigned to each, unless the
context requires otherwise: Establishment, Employer, Worker, Work, Employment
Contract, Wage, Basic Wage, and related terms as defined by the Ministry.

Article (8)
The employment contract shall be drawn up in two copies, one to be delivered to
the worker and the other to be kept by the employer. The employment relationship
may be proven by all means of evidence where no written contract exists.

Article (13)
The employment contract shall include, in particular: the name of the employer
and place of business; the name, qualifications, profession and nationality of
the worker; the date of conclusion of the contract; the date of commencement of
work; the place of work; the term of the contract; the wage and benefits; and
the probation period if any.

Article (30)
The maternity leave entitlement for a working woman shall be sixty (60) days,
comprising forty-five (45) days on full pay and fifteen (15) days on half pay,
subject to the conditions set out in the implementing regulations.

Article (43)
Either party to the employment contract may terminate it for a legitimate reason,
provided that the other party is notified in writing and work continues during
the notice period, which shall be not less than thirty (30) days and not more
than ninety (90) days. The party who fails to honour the notice period shall pay
the other party compensation in lieu of notice.

Article (51)
The worker who has completed one year or more of continuous service shall be
entitled to an end-of-service benefit calculated on the basis of the basic wage:
twenty-one (21) days' wage for each of the first five years of service, and
thirty (30) days' wage for each additional year, provided the total does not
exceed two years' wage.

Article (65)
The maximum normal working hours for workers shall be eight (8) hours per day or
forty-eight (48) hours per week. Working hours may be increased or decreased for
certain economic sectors or categories of workers pursuant to the implementing
regulations.

Article (71)
Without prejudice to categories excepted by the implementing regulations, a
worker may not be required to work more than five (5) consecutive hours without
breaks for rest, food and prayer, which are not counted within working hours.
