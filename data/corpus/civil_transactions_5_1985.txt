Article (125)
A contract is the meeting of an offer made by one of the contracting parties
with the acceptance of the other and their concurrence in such a manner as to
determine its effect on the subject matter of the contract, giving rise to the
obligations of each party towards the other in respect thereof.

Article (129)
The essential elements of a valid contract are: (1) agreement of the parties on
the essential elements of the contract; (2) a subject matter that is determined
or capable of being determined and lawful; (3) a lawful cause of obligation.

Article (246)
(1) A contract shall be performed in accordance with its terms and in a manner
consistent with the requirements of good faith. (2) A contract shall not be
limited to obligating a contracting party to its express terms but shall extend
to that which is incidental to it in accordance with law, custom, and the
nature of the transaction.

Article (257)
The basic rule in contracts is the agreement of the contracting parties and
what they have mutually obligated themselves to do under the contract. A
contract may not be revoked or modified except by mutual agreement of the
parties or by an order of the court for legally prescribed reasons.

Article (318)
A person who without lawful cause obtains a benefit at the expense of another
shall be obliged, within the limits of the benefit, to restore that benefit to
the person from whom it was obtained, or to compensate that person for the
value of the benefit obtained.

Article (390)
Where the contract is silent as to the duration of an obligation that by its
nature is continuing, the obligation shall continue for such period as is
reasonable in light of the nature of the transaction, the express purpose of
the parties, and customary practice in transactions of the same kind.

Article (905)
A confidential agent or representative who, in the course of his commission,
becomes aware of confidential information of the principal shall be bound to
preserve that information and shall not use it for his own benefit or disclose
it to third parties, except with the express authorization of the principal,
during the engagement and for a reasonable period after termination.
